Plan Your Visit

Travelling from overseas or interstate? Here's everything you need to prepare before you arrive in Sydney.

📄 Visa & Entry

Most visitors to Australia need an Electronic Travel Authority (ETA, subclass 601). You can apply online through the Australian Government's official ETA app — it's quick and usually approved within minutes.

Citizens of New Zealand can visit Australia without a visa under the Trans-Tasman travel arrangement.

Apply well in advance. Check your eligibility and visa options at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au

🌡 What to Pack

Sydney in July is winter — expect temperatures between 8–17°C. The weather can be mild and sunny during the day but chilly in the mornings and evenings.

  • Warm layers — Jumper, jacket, or fleece for cool mornings and evenings
  • Rain jacket — Winter showers are common
  • Comfortable walking shoes — Especially for temple visits and sightseeing
  • Power adapters — Australia uses Type I plugs (230V, 50Hz)
  • Sunscreen — The Australian sun is strong, even on clear winter days

💰 Currency & Payments

Australia uses the Australian Dollar (AUD). You'll find payments easy and cashless-friendly across Sydney.

  • Contactless payment — Widely accepted at shops, restaurants, and transport. Tap your card or phone almost everywhere.
  • ATMs — Available at every shopping centre, train station, and main street
  • Tipping — Not expected in Australia. Service is included in the price.

🛡 Travel Insurance

Strongly recommended for all overseas visitors. Australia's public healthcare system (Medicare) does not cover international visitors, and medical costs can be very high.

Make sure your policy covers medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and lost luggage.

⏱ Jet Lag Tips

Sydney is UTC+10 (AEST) in July. Depending on where you're travelling from, the time difference can be significant.

  • Try to arrive a day early if possible to rest and adjust
  • Stay hydrated during your flight
  • Get some sunlight on arrival to help reset your body clock

✅ Arrival Checklist

Before you leave home, make sure you have these essentials ready:

  • Passport — Valid for the duration of your stay
  • Visa / ETA confirmation — Screenshot or printout
  • Travel insurance documents — Policy number and emergency contact
  • Emergency contact card — With local and home contacts
  • Temple address saved offline — 1/3 Tasha Place, Kings Park NSW 2148

🎫 Opal Card & Tips

You'll need an Opal card or contactless debit/credit card to tap on and off all trains, buses, ferries, and light rail in Sydney.

  • Where to get one — Opal cards are available at airport stations, convenience stores, and newsagents across Sydney.
  • Top up — Online, via the Opal Travel app, or at Opal retailers.
  • Contactless — You can also tap on/off with Visa, Mastercard, or Amex (debit or credit) — no Opal card needed.
  • Daily cap — Fares are capped at $16.80/day and $50/week, so travel is affordable for multi-day visitors.
  • Sunday cap — All-day travel on Sundays is capped at just $2.80.

Staying Connected

Need a local mobile number or data while in Sydney? Here's everything you need to know.

📶 Mobile Operators

These networks have strong coverage in the Kings Park / Blacktown area:

  • Telstra — Best overall coverage across Australia. Premium pricing.
  • Optus — Strong coverage in metro Sydney. Good value plans.
  • Vodafone — Reliable in urban areas. Competitive prepaid options.

Budget carriers like Boost (Telstra network), Amaysim (Optus network), and Woolworths Mobile (Telstra network) offer cheaper prepaid plans on the same towers.

💳 Where to Buy a SIM Card

Prepaid SIM cards are widely available. No contract required — just pick one up and activate.

  • Sydney Airport — Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone kiosks in the arrivals hall.
  • Coles — Sells Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone prepaid SIMs at the checkout or mobile display.
  • Woolworths — Stocks Telstra, Optus, Vodafone, and Woolworths Mobile prepaid SIMs.
  • Aldi — Sells ALDImobile prepaid SIMs (runs on the Telstra network) — great value data plans.
  • Officeworks, JB Hi-Fi, Australia Post — Wide range of prepaid SIM options.

You'll need your passport to activate a prepaid SIM in Australia (ID verification is required by law).

📡 eSIM

If your phone supports eSIM, you can set up a plan before you even land:

  • Telstra — eSIM available via the My Telstra app or in-store.
  • Optus — eSIM available online or via the My Optus app.
  • Vodafone — eSIM available via the My Vodafone app.
  • Airalo / Holafly — Travel eSIM providers with Australia data plans you can buy online before departure. Data-only (no local number).

eSIM is supported on iPhone XS and newer, Google Pixel 3 and newer, Samsung Galaxy S20 and newer, and most recent flagship phones. Check your phone's settings to confirm compatibility.

Practical Info

A few handy things to know about daily life in Australia to help you feel right at home.

🙌 Customs & Etiquette

Australians are friendly, laid-back, and informal. Don't be surprised if someone greets you with a casual "G'day!" — it's a warm hello.

  • Queue patiently — Australians value fairness and orderly queues
  • A smile and "thank you" go a long way
  • First names are used freely, even in professional settings

💵 Tipping

Tipping is not expected in Australia. Service staff are paid a fair wage, and there is no obligation to leave a tip at restaurants, cafes, or taxis.

Some restaurants may add a surcharge on weekends and public holidays (typically 10–15%) — this is clearly noted on the menu and covers higher staff wages on those days.

💧 Drinking Water

Tap water is safe to drink throughout Sydney and across Australia. You can fill your bottle from any tap — no need to buy bottled water.

Many public spaces, parks, and shopping centres have drinking water fountains.

📱 Useful Apps

Download these before you arrive to make getting around Sydney easy:

  • Opal Travel — Official public transport app for Sydney trains, buses, ferries, and light rail
  • TripView — Real-time train and bus timetables (a local favourite)
  • Google Maps — Navigation and public transport directions
  • Uber — Rideshare, widely available across Sydney
  • WhatsApp — Stay in touch with family and event organisers

🇦🇺 Australian Phrases

You might hear some uniquely Australian expressions during your visit. Here's a quick guide:

  • "Arvo" — Afternoon
  • "No worries" — You're welcome / it's all good
  • "Servo" — Petrol station (gas station)
  • "Macca's" — McDonald's
  • "Brekkie" — Breakfast
  • "Reckon" — Think / believe

📅 Public Holidays

There are no public holidays during 4–12 July 2026, so shops, transport, and services will operate on regular schedules.

However, NAIDOC Week (5–12 July) is celebrated city-wide with cultural events, markets, and community gatherings honouring Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples.

Can't make it in person? Follow along from anywhere in the world.

How to Chromecast to Your TV

Cast the live stream of Sangathan Parva events directly to your television:

  1. Ensure your Chromecast device and phone/laptop are on the same Wi-Fi network.
  2. Open the live stream link (to be shared closer to the event) in Google Chrome on your phone or laptop.
  3. Tap the Cast icon (rectangle with Wi-Fi waves) in your browser or video player.
  4. Select your Chromecast device from the list.
  5. The stream will appear on your TV. Use your phone to control playback.

From a Mac

  1. Open Google Chrome on your Mac.
  2. Go to the live stream link.
  3. Click the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner of Chrome.
  4. Select "Cast, save, and share" then "Cast..."
  5. Choose your Chromecast device from the list.
  6. To cast your entire screen, click "Sources" and select "Cast screen".

Other Ways to Watch

  • Apple AirPlay — Tap the AirPlay icon and select your Apple TV or AirPlay-compatible smart TV.
  • Smart TV browser — Open the live stream link directly in your smart TV's built-in web browser.
  • HDMI cable — Connect your laptop to your TV with an HDMI cable for a simple, reliable connection.
