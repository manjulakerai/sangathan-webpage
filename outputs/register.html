Public Transport

🚆 Train

Kings Park is served by the T1 Western Line. The nearest stations are:

  • Marayong Station — closest to the Mandir, approx. 700 m walk
  • Seven Hills Station — next closest, well connected
  • Blacktown Station — major interchange with express services from the CBD

🚍 Bus

Local bus routes serving Kings Park and surrounding suburbs:

  • 734 — Riverstone to Blacktown via Kings Park
  • 743 — Kings Langley to Blacktown via Kings Park & Marayong
  • 702 — Seven Hills to Blacktown
  • 711 — Blacktown to Parramatta via Constitution Hill
  • 718 — Kings Langley to Seven Hills

🎫 Opal Card & Tips

You'll need an Opal card or contactless debit/credit card to tap on and off all trains, buses, ferries, and light rail in Sydney.

  • Where to get one — Opal cards are available at airport stations, convenience stores, and newsagents across Sydney.
  • Top up — Online, via the Opal Travel app, or at Opal retailers.
  • Contactless — You can also tap on/off with Visa, Mastercard, or Amex (debit or credit) — no Opal card needed.
  • Daily cap — Fares are capped at $16.80/day and $50/week, so travel is affordable for multi-day visitors.
  • Sunday cap — All-day travel on Sundays is capped at just $2.80.

Car Hire

If you'd like to explore Sydney at your own pace, rental cars are available from these locations:

  • Sydney Airport — All major rental companies (Hertz, Avis, Budget, Europcar, Thrifty) are located in the arrivals terminal.
  • Parramatta — Several rental agencies near the CBD, closer to the Mandir.
  • Online pre-booking — Recommended for the best rates. Compare on CarHire.com.au, VroomVroomVroom, or book direct.

An international driving permit (IDP) is required for overseas visitors along with your home country licence. Driving is on the left side of the road in Australia.
